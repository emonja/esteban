YO SOY

Manual & Development Log — v01

1. Project Overview

YO SOY is an experiential pedagogical system designed to explore individuality, community, agency, control, and emergence through sound.

Rather than explaining these concepts verbally, the system places participants inside them. Each participant becomes a continuous sonic presence in a shared physical space.

No performance is required. No verbal introduction is requested. Sound exists. Participation is unavoidable once entered.

2. Pedagogical Intent

This project was developed in response to several recurring problems in educational contexts:

  • First-day introductions that expose or pressure students
  • Superficial inclusion rituals
  • The privatization of public space by personal devices
  • Students’ high sensitivity to external stimuli and systems

Instead of asking students to speak, the system asks them to exist.

There is no evaluation, no correctness, and no failure. The experience becomes the material for reflection afterward.

3. Conceptual Structure

Stage-Based Design

The project is structured in stages. Each stage modifies the relationship between the individual and the system.

Stage 1 — Individuals in a Bunch (Current)

  • Each participant uses their own device
  • Sound is emitted through device speakers (no headphones)
  • Each sound is different
  • All sounds share the same physical space

The result is a layered, imperfect, spatial sonic crowd. There is no synchronization and no hierarchy.

Later stages will progressively remove individual control and introduce collective behavior.

4. System Architecture

4.1 index.html — Entry & Conditioning

The entry interface is responsible for registering presence and establishing the conditions of the experience.

  • ID-based entry (non-personal, secular identifiers)
  • No authentication
  • Explicit instruction to use speakers
  • Silent progression through screens

The questionnaire does not collect personal data. It collects symbolic parameters only.

4.2 pulse.html — Sonic Instrument

The pulse interface turns each participant into a continuous sonic agent.

  • Web Audio API oscillator
  • ADSR envelope
  • BPM-based triggering
  • Waveform selection
  • Waveform and envelope visualization

The instrument is identical for everyone, but configured differently.

5. Questionnaire → Sound Mapping

Subjective states are translated into sound parameters:

  • Internal rhythm → BPM
  • Thought texture → Waveform
  • Emotional energy → Frequency

This translation is symbolic, not diagnostic. The student is encoding presence, not describing identity.

7. Audio Design Constraints

Several constraints are deliberate:

  • No headphones — forces shared space
  • Continuous sound — presence cannot be paused
  • Minimal controls — avoids performance mindset
  • Neutral visuals — informational, not expressive

The instrument is not meant to sound good. It is meant to exist.

9. Ethical Notes

The system intentionally creates discomfort:

  • Sound cannot be ignored
  • Contribution is involuntary
  • Individual actions affect the group

These conditions mirror real-world shared systems. The discussion happens after the experience, not before.

10. Current Position

Stage 1 is conceptually complete and technically functional. The system is stable enough for classroom deployment and discussion, but not yet locked for scale or Stage 2.

11. Handoff Note

If you are resuming this project:

  • Do not over-explain before running it
  • Do not clean up discomfort
  • Let silence and sound do the work

Your role is not to guide the experience, but to hold the space afterward.
